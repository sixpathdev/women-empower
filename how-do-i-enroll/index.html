We Are Here For You

NOTICE: In an effort to combat the spread of COVID-19, we have implemented strict social distancing and safety procedures at our facility. Due to adjusted capacity, anyone interested in attending a program orientation must RSVP. Our mission throughout this crisis is to continue to be a trusted resource and provide comprehensive services for women and children experiencing homelessness. If you would like to stay informed about orientation information, enrollment, and resources available please email Yara our Program Services Coordinator at yara@womens-empowerment.org.
Women’s Empowerment nine-week, Job-readiness and Empowerment program is open to women* over the age of 18 who are experiencing (or at risk of) housing insecurity. If you or someone you know is experiencing homelessness, including:
● Staying with family or in a motel
● Living outside or in a car
● Staying in a shelter
● Couch-surfing
● Transitioning out of prison
● Facing eviction

…or if you or someone you know is at risk of any of these, please come to an orientation!

*People who identify as non-binary genders and feel they belong in this community of cis and trans women are invited/welcome to participate in our programs

Attending an orientation is a requirement for acceptance to the program.

When? Three to four orientations are scheduled prior to each nine-week job-readiness and empowerment session. Orientations usually last about 2 ½ hours.  Please come early since there is limited space available. All you need to bring is yourself!

Next Orientations (PLEASE RSVP TO ATTEND):

  • Monday, September 27th, from 10 a.m. to 12:30 p.m.
  • Wednesday, September 29th, from 10 a.m. to 12:30 p.m.
  • Monday, October 4th, from 10 a.m. to 12:30 p.m.
  • Wednesday, October 6th, from 10 a.m. to 12:30 p.m.

Please RSVP to 916-669-2307 if you would like to attend. Download Orientation Flyer

Where? Orientations take place at the Women’s Empowerment facilities located at 1590 North A Street, Sacramento, CA 95811.

What? At orientation you will have the chance to meet some of the staff, and find out more about Women’s Empowerment and what our program entails. This is a great opportunity to find out if Women’s Empowerment is right for you.

If you have children, please note that we provide on-site childcare for them in our Child Development Center.

If you have any questions, you can speak with a Women’s Empowerment representative at 916-669-2307 for more information.

WOMEN’S EMPOWERMENT

The mission of Women’s Empowerment is to educate and empower women who are experiencing homelessness with the skills and confidence necessary to get a job, maintain a healthy lifestyle, and regain a home for themselves and their children.
