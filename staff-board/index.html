We Are A Team

Our team is committed to ensuring women and children experiencing homelessness have the tools and resources necessary to achieve their dreams. Please click on staff members’ names to learn more about them and their work with Women’s Empowerment! Need to speak with a staff member but you are not sure who to reach out to? Simply call 916-669-2307 or email support@womens-empowerment.org and someone will help!

Meet Our Staff

EARL BEAN - SAFETY MONITOR

Earl Bean’s passion is helping strengthen his community, and he says one of his greatest talents is supporting others as they navigate their life journeys. Having worked at Women’s Empowerment since 2013, Earl has been inspired by the transformations he has witnessed as women graduate from the program. He enjoys ensuring that everyone at the office is safe, and that our program operates smoothly.

When he is not at Women’s Empowerment, Earl loves spending time outdoors fishing, barbequing, visiting parks, and playing dominoes with his friends and family.

LAUREN BROCE - AMERICORPS VISTA & SOCIAL MEDIA COORDINATOR

Lauren Broce graduated from Kansas State University in 2020 with Degrees in both Mass Communications and Spanish, as well as a Certificate in Travel and Tourism Promotion. Her passion for community engagement and her background in social media and communications motivated her to search for an AmeriCorps position in the nonprofit sector. She started her year of AmeriCorps VISTA service with Women’s Empowerment in March of 2021. As the Social Media and Communications Coordinator, Lauren manages WE’s social media platforms and creates sustainable processes to streamline public communications. Empowered by Women’s Empowerment’s mission, she is inspired every day as she hears and shares with the community the powerful stories of the strong and courageous women WE serves.

Originally from Miami, Lauren takes any chance she can get to be in or near the ocean. In her spare time, you can find her reading by the river in her beloved hammock, exploring California, or over-caffeinating on Cuban coffee.

916-669-2307×106 | Lauren@womens-empowerment.org

LISA CULP - EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR

By the young age of 13, Lisa knew she wanted to make a difference in the community, and did so by helping those who founded Loaves & Fishes bring sandwiches to the homeless near the railroad tracks. Lisa studied both at UCLA and University of Grenoble in France, and received her degree from UC Davis. A long-time activist and voice for those less fortunate, Lisa spent 10 years after college working in Nicaragua while living on dirt floors. After seeing poverty abroad, Lisa chose to return home to raise her son. She continued working with those struggling to survive by creating various programs with Loaves & Fishes and Quinn Cottages. One of the projects Lisa designed grew into today’s Women’s Empowerment. After working more than 10 years on local issues regarding homelessness and as an employee of Loaves & Fishes, Lisa currently serves as Executive Director of the now independent nonprofit, Women’s Empowerment, which earned Nonprofit of the Year in 2009. In 2008, she received the Woman of the Year Award from the National Association of Business Women for her vision in founding the organization. In 2010, she earned the Ruby Award for her efforts to serve the homeless, a distinction granted on a regional level. Lisa speaks Spanish, German, and French.

916-669-2307 | Lisa@womens-empowerment.org

TRACIE DAVIDSON - OPERATIONS & SOCIAL ENTERPRISE MANAGER

Tracie joined the team in July 2019. She always felt it was important to serve her community and while living in San Francisco, spent all of her free time volunteering with the homeless community. She found this so rewarding that she decided she wanted to do this full time and moved to Sacramento in search of a nonprofit that would align with her goals and values. When she found Women’s Empowerment, she knew she found her home. She started out as a volunteer in the WE – Lending Library and as a career mentor. After a short few months volunteering she saw an opportunity for a Social Enterprise Manager and given her management experience and love of working with these women she knew this would be the perfect fit. She applied and got the position, and now has the privilege of working with WE graduates to help create the Get a Job Kit, a social enterprise that helps job seekers across the country find employment.

916-231-1394 | Tracie@womens-empowerment.org

KAITLYN MONCK - HOUSING SPECIALIST

Kaitlyn joined the Women’s Empowerment team in 2020 as a Housing Specialist. She holds a Bachelors of Science in Criminal Justice, as well as a Bachelors of Arts in History with a minor in Political Science from Waldorf University.  She is currently working on her law degree at University of the Pacific McGeorge with the goal of one day providing affordable legal services to women and children.

While completing multiple missions to Guatemala, China, South Africa and Swaziland, Kaitlyn became aware of the many injustices that existed for persons who were unable to change their circumstances. Upon returning to the United States she started to notice that similar infrastructure was in place, where people, unaware of their rights were being re-victimized, not supported, and placed in a seemingly unescapable cycle of abuse and poverty. It was then that Kaitlyn felt as though her purpose was clear, so she began to pursue education and career routes that would allow her to change that narrative.

Prior to joining Women’s Empowerment Kaitlyn worked as a law clerk for a firm that practiced Landlord-Tenant Law. Specifically, they specialized in subsidized housing and low-income mobile home and RV communities in the greater California area.  Because of this experience Kaitlyn is well versed in state and federal Fair Housing Laws, and has a deep understanding of the rights afforded tenants in California.

In her free time she enjoys playing volleyball, going for hikes, and soaking up all that Northern California has to offer.

EMILY BERGER - JOB DEVELOPER/EMPLOYMENT SPECIALIST

Emily received her Master’s in Social Work with a concentration in Health and Wellness from the University of Denver in 2016. With a focus on Trauma Informed Care and Diversity, Equity and Inclusion, she has been working with disenfranchised youth and adults for the past eight years. Her work has included substance abuse treatment, addressing access to healthcare, youth and adult employment services and even taking marginalized youth white water rafting. Emily joined Women’s Empowerment in 2019 where she works to break cycles of intergenerational poverty by facilitating learning about how to obtain and maintain employment.

GLORIA BURROUGHS - COMMUNITY PARTNERSHIPS COORDINATOR

Gloria joined Women’s Empowerment in July of 2021 after making a big move from South Carolina to be part of something bigger than herself. She graduated from the University of South Carolina where she majored in public relations and minored in marketing. During her coursework, Gloria gained knowledge of fundraising, campaigns, public speaking, and the general inner workings of a nonprofit. After completing her schooling and volunteering, she knew that she wanted to pursue a career in the nonprofit industry.

After graduating, Gloria moved to the west coast and dedicated one year to being AmeriCorps VISTA at United Way of Northern Nevada and the Sierra. Working as their marketing outreach coordinator, Gloria planned and assisted with campaigns and fundraising, generated content for their newsletters and social media, and helped give out funds to other nonprofits and families during the height of the pandemic.

In her free time, Gloria enjoys playing volleyball, dancing, trying new foods, and traveling.

ZOE FISHMAN - DEVELOPMENT MANAGER

Zoe Fishman joined the Women’s Empowerment team in March of 2019 bringing a plethora of experience in both the private and nonprofit sectors. Skilled in campaign development, relationship building, marketing, and event fundraising, Zoe has a creative and innovative approach to the continuous advancement of community engagement and philanthropy.

Zoe’s love of nonprofit development began with her position as Community Events Coordinator for Sunburst Projects, an organization that serves women and families impacted by HIV/AIDS. Passionate about strengthening communities and bettering the world through her work, Zoe is inspired daily by people (donors, volunteers, advocates, and community partners) who have such a tremendous capacity to make a difference, and who give so generously with their resources, time, and heart!

Prior to joining the nonprofit sector, Zoe was the production coordinator for an event planning company, producing large scale events across the United States, such as San Francisco Pride, Lesbians Who Tech (Pavilion), San Francisco Cinco De Mayo, and Pride Fort Lauderdale. Outside of work, she serves on the board of two local non-profits, is a dog lover, food fanatic, and frequent road-tripper.

ALLISON VICKREY-KAO - SOCIAL WORK INTERN

Allison Vickrey-Kao is a current master’s in clinical counseling student with Alliant University. She holds a bachelor’s degree in psychology from Ashford University. Allison comes to Women’s Empowerment with 10 years of professional experience including working with domestic violence offenders and mandated populations. In partnership with the Sacramento County Office of Education and Sacramento and Yolo County probation departments, Allison has taught domestic violence classes and facilitated Batterers Intervention Programs in local jails and day reporting centers. She has also worked for Empower Yolo as a Domestic Violence Peer Counselor, Batterers Intervention Facilitator, and Sexual Assault Response Team Advocate. She is excited to be a part of the WE team as she completes her clinical practicum and internship through March 2022. In her free time, she enjoys cooking, spending time at the lake, and being with her children.

WOMEN’S EMPOWERMENT

The mission of Women’s Empowerment is to educate and empower women who are experiencing homelessness with the skills and confidence necessary to get a job, maintain a healthy lifestyle, and regain a home for themselves and their children.
