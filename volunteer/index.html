NOTICE: In an effort to fight the spread of COVID-19 and follow directives set forth by Sacramento County health officials, we have implemented strict safety procedures at our facility. We have implemented a mandatory vaccination policy effective 7/14/21 requiring COVID-19 vaccination(s) for all in-person volunteers (i.e. anyone volunteering at our facility or in-person with our participants) . Unvaccinated individuals are welcome to participate in our virtual volunteer opportunities. Our mission throughout this crisis is to continue to be a trusted resource and provide comprehensive services for women and children experiencing homelessness. For inquiries, please contact Community Partnerships Coordinator, Gloria Burroughs, at gloria@womens-empowerment.org.
Volunteers are the backbone of Women’s Empowerment and are essential to our work. By giving with time, energy, and heart, volunteers truly make an impact for the women and children we serve. Thank you for your interest in volunteering with us. There are a wide variety of volunteer opportunities at Women’s Empowerment.

Ongoing Volunteer opportunities include:

• Career Mentor
• Program Workshop/Classroom Assistant
• Childcare Assistant
• Volunteer Receptionists
• Clothing Closet Attendant
• Thrift Store Driver
• Teacher/Facilitator/Workshop Instructor
• Ambassador/Fundraising
• Special Events/3rd Party Events
• Lending Library Facilitator

VOLUNTEERING FAQ’S

1. Are there any evening and/or weekend opportunities?

The majority of our volunteer opportunities are during business hours from 8:30am-3:30pm. We do have event specific opportunities during our Gala planning period and the Winter Holidays.

3. How old do I have to be?

In our Child Development Center we allow volunteers ages 15+. Generally for all other opportunities, we ask that volunteers are 18+. We do occasionally make exceptions on a case by case basis. If you have any questions regarding age, please email Community Partnerships Coordinator, Gloria Burroughs at gloria@womens-empowerment.org

5. Do you have volunteer opportunities for groups?

We do have some opportunities for groups. For group volunteering inquiries please email Community Partnerships Coordinator, Gloria Burroughs, at gloria@womens-empowerment.org.

2. Are you looking for specific skills?

The majority of our volunteers are caring, supportive individuals who believe in our mission and are looking to pay it forward and make a difference. There sometimes are special requirements for specific volunteer areas, but we will inform you of those at the orientation.

4. I have court mandated volunteer hours I need to fill. Can I fill them at Women’s Empowerment?

This is done on a case by case basis. We may have some opportunities that may work for you, but do not assume you will be able to fill your hours at Women’s Empowerment. Contact us at gloria@womens-empowerment.org or 916.669.2307 ext 117.

6. Does Women’s Empowerment offer internship opportunities?

Social Work Interns: We generally work with Social Work Students (MSW II) through CSU Sacramento. If you are qualified and interested in learning more about this opportunity please contact Social Worker, Renae Garcia, at Renae@womens-empowerment.org.

If you think volunteering with Women’s Empowerment is right for you, here is how you can get started:
1. Attend a Volunteer Orientation. We do require all volunteers to attend an orientation, it is a great way for us to get to know one another, and establish the right volunteer fit among our many needs and opportunities. Volunteer Orientations take place via zoom on the third Wednesday of each month from 4:00pm – 5:15 pm. To RSVP to attend a volunteer orientation, simply enter your information in the fillable form on this page or email gloria@womens-empowerment.org.

2. Complete the Volunteer-Information-App
3. Sign the Volunteer Agreement Form
4. Once you have attended a volunteer orientation and completed the required information and agreement forms, we will reach out to get you started volunteering!

WOMEN’S EMPOWERMENT

The mission of Women’s Empowerment is to educate and empower women who are experiencing homelessness with the skills and confidence necessary to get a job, maintain a healthy lifestyle, and regain a home for themselves and their children.
